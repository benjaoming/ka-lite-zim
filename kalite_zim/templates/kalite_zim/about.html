{% trans "About this project" %}

Khan Academy can be visited on www.khanacademy.org and there you can read much more about how they are publishing free educational materials for the whole world.

Meanwhile, the distribution of this offline version, has been made possible through data packaged by Learning Equality, an organization working to promote free online contents such as Khan Academy beyond the reach of internet (broadband) etc.

Distribution is done with videos, exercises, and interactive tools for teachers and students through the KA Lite project, soon to be re-branded Kolibri.

The reason why it has been created as a .zim file, was through a hackathon in Berlin, Jan. 2016, in which Kiwix and Learning Equality people came together. We want to see that the people distributing offline contents collaborate on common standards and platforms, and as such we are happy that data from one offline application (KA Lite) is able to ender the realm of other applications throught the OpenZim format.

In the future, we hope to see many more platforms both read and write the OpenZim format, and that other projects will generate more APIs and extensions for this purpose.

More over, we encourage developers to join aboard a growing movement of content liberators who seek not only to provide free and wonderful privileges of education to those with fast internet access, but also to those without!

Berlin, January 10th 2016
